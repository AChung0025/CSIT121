Final Reflection

While working on this project, I’ve noticed a significant improvement in my understanding of HTML and CSS. However, I still struggle with JavaScript. It’s been exciting to finally grasp how these languages work—it almost feels like I’ve learned a new language I can now write and read. For this project, I focused on the visual design and how to communicate my ideas through appearance, applying what I’ve learned in class.
Looking back at my earlier labs, it’s clear I didn’t fully understand how to structure or position elements correctly. Through this course, I’ve learned how to customize elements to achieve the look I want. While I still need more practice, I’m confident I’ll get there. I’ve left my labs unchanged to highlight my progress—the early ones lack responsive design, while the later ones show clear improvement.
This journey hasn’t been easy, but I now have a stronger grasp of what goes on behind the scenes in both front-end and back-end web development. I’ve also learned about various web standards and how to apply them in my work. JavaScript has been my biggest challenge. Even though its syntax resembles other programming languages, I still find it confusing. I’ve been trying different approaches to understand it better and hope to improve with continued practice.
